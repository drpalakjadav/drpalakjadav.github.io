    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is Egg Freezing?

    Egg freezing (Oocyte Cryopreservation) is a process where a woman's eggs are extracted, frozen, and stored for future use. This allows women to preserve their fertility for later, whether for medical reasons (like cancer treatment) or personal choice (career, finding the right partner). The frozen eggs can be thawed and used for IVF when ready to conceive.

    What is Embryo Freezing?

    Embryo freezing (Embryo Cryopreservation) is the process of preserving fertilized embryos at sub-zero temperatures for future use. During IVF, multiple embryos are often created, and the best quality embryos not transferred immediately can be frozen and stored. These frozen embryos can later be thawed and transferred in subsequent cycles, eliminating the need for repeated egg retrieval and reducing treatment costs.
